Cryptids of the Americas

The Chupacabra

  • Country: Mexico/Latin America
  • First Sighting: 1995
  • Type: Reptilian-like

The Chupacabra is a legendary creature rumored to inhabit arts of the Americas, with the earliest reported sightings in Puerto Rico before spreading to Mexico and other Latin American regions.

It is infamous for attacking livestock, especially goats, and is said to drain them of blood. Witnesses often describe the Chupacabra as a reptilian or alien-looking creature, standing on two legs, with leathery or scaly gray-green skin and a row of spines or quills running down its back. Despite numerous sightings and alleged evidence, its existence remains unproven and widely debated.

Mothman

  • Country: United States
  • First Sighting: 1966
  • Type: Winged Humanoid

Mothman is a cryptid reported primarily in Point Pleasant, West Virginia, first sighted in 1966 near an old munitions site known as the "TNT area." Witnesses describe a tall, man-sized creature with enormous wings, no visible arms, and glowing red eyes that shine like reflectors.

Many sightings occurred just before the tragic collapse of the Silver Bridge in 1967, leading some to believe the Mothman is a harbinger of doom. Its story has since become a major part of local folklore, inspiring books, movies, and an annual festival.

Tshenkutshen

  • Country: Ecuador
  • First Sighting: 1998
  • Type: Cat-like Marsupial

The tshenkutshen, also spelt tsenkutsen and tsenkutsenku together meaning "seven ribbons [rainbow] tiger" or howler monkey tiger is a cryptid reported from Ecuador.

The Tshenkutshen resembles a slightly smaller jaguar but with key differences.
