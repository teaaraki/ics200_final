Cryptids of Asia

Ningen

  • Country: Japan / Antarctica
  • First Sighting: 1960s
  • Type: Oceanic Humanoid / Cephalopod

Over the past few years, rumors have circulated in Japan about the existence of polar Humanoid Life Forms inhabiting the icy waters of the Antarctic called Ningen (人間, "Ningen", meaning "human"). Reportedly observed on multiple occasions by crew members of government-operated "whale research" ships, these so-called Ningen are said to be completely white in color with an estimated length of 20 to 30 meters. Eyewitnesses describe them having a human-like shape, often with legs, arms, and even five-fingered hands. Sometimes they are described as having fins or a large mermaid-like tail instead of legs, or even tentacles and cephalopod arms and sometimes they are armless small bipedal humanoids, similar to the Fresno Nightcrawlers.

Most of the photo evidence of the Ningen was either hoaxed or artistic interpretations. According to Wikipedia.org, the Ningen first appeared on the Internet in the mid-2000s and was most likely an Internet hoax.

The Yeti

  • Country: Himalayan Mountains
  • First Mention: 1832
  • Type: Humanoid Primate

The Yeti is known as the "Abominable Snowman" since it hails from the cold climate of the Himalayas — and because its name loosely translates to "metoh-kangmi," which means "man-bear snowman." However, this elusive creature doesn't look like a snowman at all.

Covered in brown fur, the Yeti reportedly resembles a human-gorilla hybrid or possibly even a bear, and it stands more than 11 feet (3.3 meters) tall.

Mongolian Death Worm

  • Country: Mongolia
  • First Sighting: 11th Century
  • Type: Worm-like

The Mongolian Death Worm's native name, Olgoi-Khorkhoi, means "intestine worm" due to its red, blood-like color and size. It has been described by many people as being from 2–7 feet long and having the ability to spit out corrosive yellow saliva and generate blasts of electricity. However, this latter power is thought to be folkloric by Gobi nomads.

Nobody is entirely sure what the worm actually is. Experts are certain it is not a real worm because the Gobi Desert is too hot for annelids. Some suggest it might be a skink, but they have legs and scales, unlike the limbless, smooth-bodied descriptions. The most likely explanation is a new species of amphisbaenia or worm-lizard — a group of burrowing reptiles.

Ahool

  • Country: Indonesia (Java)
  • First Mention: 1920's
  • Type: Giant Bat-like Creature

The Ahool is a cryptid said to inhabit the rainforests of Java. It is described as a giant bat or flying primate with an enormous wingspan—reportedly up to 10 feet wide—and a face that resembles a monkey or flat-nosed ape. Its name comes from the distinctive cry it is said to make: “Ahooool!”

Some believe it could be a previously unknown species of bat or a surviving pterosaur, though others think it may just be a misidentified large owl or fruit bat. Despite its terrifying size and appearance, reports of attacks are rare—most sightings are brief glimpses at dusk or night.
