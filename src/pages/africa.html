Mokele-Mbembe

  • Country: Republic of the Congo
  • First Sighting: 1929, but reports of a large animal date back to the 18th century
  • Type: Living dinosaur

Mokele-Mbembe is said to be a massive creature living in remote parts of Central Africa. Sightings are often around the Congo River, where locals describe it as a large amphibious animal with a bulky body, a long neck and tail, and a somewhat small head, a description which has long suggested a sauropod dinosaur.


There are many theories that Mokele-Mbembe is just a case of mistaken identity. It simply could be the case of a different type of reptile that lives in the area.

Kongamato

  • Country: Zambia / Angola / Congo
  • First Sighting: 1920's
  • Type: insert

Kongamato, meaning “overturner of boats,” is a flying cryptid reported in the swampy regions of central Africa. Descriptions closely resemble a living pterosaur: leathery wings, long beak, and no feathers.


Early reports from Western explorers noted that locals recognized pterosaur illustrations as the creature they feared. It is said to attack boats and intrude on human settlements. Despite this, no physical evidence has been found to confirm its existence.

Nandi Bear

  • Country: Kenya
  • First Sighting: Early 1900's
  • Type: Carnivorous Mammal

The Nandi Bear, named after the Nandi people of Kenya, is described as a large, shaggy, hyena- or bear-like animal with a sloping back, powerful limbs, and fearsome strength. It's known in local folklore as a man-eater, feared for its aggressive nature.


While no scientific evidence confirms its existence, colonial-era reports and continued local legends have fueled speculation that the creature may be a surviving prehistoric animal, a misidentified hyena, or even a case of mass folklore. Some believe it could be a relic population of short-faced bears or chalicotheres.

Man-Eating Tree

  • Country: Madagascar
  • FirstSighting: 1874
  • Type: Carnivorous Plant

In 1881 German explorer "Carl Liche" wrote an account in the South Australian Register of encountering a sacrifice performed by the "Mkodo" tribe of Madagascar. Writing:


"The slender delicate palps, with the fury of starved serpents, quivered a moment over her head, then as if with instinct and demonic intelligence fastened upon her in sudden coils round and round her neck and arms; then while her awful screams and yet more awful laughter rose wildly to be instantly strangled down again into a gurgling moan, the tendrils one after another, like great green serpents, with brutal energy and infernal rapidity, rose, retracted themselves, and wrapped her about in fold after fold, ever-tightening with cruel swiftness and savage tenacity of anacondas fastening upon their prey."
