Tshenkutshen

  • Country: Ecuador
  • First Sighting: 1998
  • Type: Cat-like Marsupial

The tshenkutshen, also spelt tsenkutsen and tsenkutsenku (both names are sometimes given the suffix "-yawá," together meaning "seven ribbons [rainbow] tiger") or howler monkey tiger is a cryptid reported from Ecuador.

The Tshenkutshen resembles a slightly smaller jaguar but with key differences. Its chest has red, yellow, and white stripes, like a toucan, while the rest of its coat is either black or white with spots, similar to the cryptid shiashia-yawá. It also has monkey-like, clawed hands, strong forelimbs, a shoulder hump, and reportedly longer canines than a jaguar.

Described as highly aggressive and mostly arboreal, it leaps between trees with ease and is considered the most dangerous jungle animal by the Shuar people. It features in Shuar poetry as a symbol of ferocity and is said to live in riverside trees, sometimes hunting in pairs. Folklore varies on its movement, with some stories claiming it never walks on land
